Divisions of Bangladesh

Explore the diverse regions of Bangladesh

Capital Division

Dhaka Division is the most populous and economically significant region in Bangladesh. It includes the capital city, Dhaka, which serves as a hub for commerce, culture, education, and government.

Key Features: Capital city, Economic hub, Historical landmarks

Major Landmarks
  • National Parliament House
  • Lalbagh Fort
  • Ahsan Manzil

Port City Division

Chattogram Division lies in southeastern Bangladesh and is home to the country's largest seaport. Chattogram city is a major industrial and commercial center.

Key Features: Seaport, Hill tracts, Longest sea beach

Tourist Attractions
  • Cox's Bazar (world's longest natural sea beach)
  • Rangamati
  • Bandarban

Agricultural Division

Rangpur Division is in the northwestern part of Bangladesh, known for agriculture and cultural heritage. It produces crops like potatoes, rice, and vegetables.

Key Features: Agricultural hub, Historical sites, Educational institutions

Notable Places
  • Tajhat Palace
  • Begum Rokeya University
  • Kantajew Temple

Mangrove Division

Khulna Division lies in southwestern Bangladesh and is famous for the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest. Khulna city is an industrial center.

Key Features: Sundarbans, Mangrove forest, Industrial center

Important Sites
  • Sundarbans Mangrove Forest
  • Mongla Port
  • Sixty Dome Mosque (Bagerhat)

Silk Division

Rajshahi Division is known for its educational institutions, silk industry, and agricultural productivity. The city is often called the "Silk City."

Key Features: Silk production, Mango orchards, Educational hub

Cultural Sites
  • Puthia Temple Complex
  • Varendra Research Museum
  • Padma Riverfront
